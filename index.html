A Field Report on Computer Science

The studies of technology can be broken down into several areas, including, but not limited to, computer science, software engineering, and information technology. Computer science is the science of teaching machines how to store, execute, and display data. Software engineering is a specific branch of computer science; it is the design, development, and testing of application code. Information technology is not as constructive as Software engineering; it is the organization and interpretation of information stored in machines. Other branches include Digital engineering, the design and development of electronic components, and Integrated Circuit engineering, the development of computer chips. Computer science encompasses all of these fields because each field involves understanding how to make inanimate objects preform logic using physics.

I am most interested in Software engineering, more specifically, Cyber-Security. As our society progresses into new generations of technology, new threats will be formulated and cause massive difficulties in reliable internet. This will create a high demand for young engineering graduates and comes with good starting salaries. Aside from the obvious, the reason I wish to fight Cybercrime is so I know that the online world becomes a safer place for me and everyone else. Computer science will help me become the programmer I want to be by not only teaching me how to create a code that hackers can get around using the backdoor, but by understanding the logic of computers and humans together, so I can have my 64-bit bloodhound wait for them behind the backdoor. In a bigger context, Computer science, along with other related courses, is only a catalyst in education; an engineer truly reaches great outcomes by learning how to expand his or her thought process. On that note, I would not recommend any scientist or graduate to mimic the process of a robot or a CPU.

How this page is User friendly

Disabilities that user may have that could prevent optimal use of a website include vision, low coordinal movment and lack of computer drives (i.e. mouse). This websie has been designed to meed the demands of those in need. The text font has been increased to a large print to allow those with troubled vision to see better. There is also no contrast with any elements or texts. Users may also lack speedy motor skills. To meet the demand for this, animations move slower than normal and these users may read at a pace they wish to have. The text should be easy and understandable to read. For those users that lack certain tools and only have a keyboard, all the necessary links to other pages are binded with keyboard shortcuts. All navigation remains on the same page to provide an easy access back to the home page and to prevent tab overflow. For users running their browsers without javascript or up-to-date software, friendly alternatives have been added to the webpage.
